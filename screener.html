2 Daily Routine — Mon–Fri (10–15 min · 8:00–9:15 AM ET)
  • Open your 2–3 watchlist names only Nothing else. Ignore everything LJ, Twitter, or Discord is talking about unless it matches a name already on your list.
  • Ask: has price reached my entry trigger? LJ trigger: price breaking and holding through your key S/R level (drawn on the daily chart), OR pulling back to S/R and holding. Not "close to it." At it and holding. SMC confirms: price in Discount zone (longs) or Premium zone (shorts) · BOS/CHoCH in your direction · ideally an Order Block tap. SmartTrend Pro's Entry signal = pull-the-trigger.
  • Confirm setup is still valid 4H trend still intact? All 4 indicators (MA ribbon, RSI, MACD, Stoch RSI) still aligned? No earnings this week within your DTE? If all yes → execute per plan at or after open.

  • Adjust stop only — never move it against you As price moves in your favor, trail your stop up. Never move a stop further away from your entry to "give it more room." That's how small losses become large ones.

⚡ Partner Setup · Max 3 DTE · Pre-market + Momentum
⚠️ Scalping requires active screen time from 9:00–10:30 AM ET minimum. Options with ≤3 DTE decay extremely fast. Size small. Losses compound quickly if stops aren't mechanical.
Night Before / Early Morning Prep — Before 8:00 AM
  • Night before (after 4:00 PM) — earnings calendar Check which names are reporting earnings before open the next day. These are either prime candidates (if catalyst is strong) or names to avoid entirely. Use Barchart Earnings Watch or Webull calendar.
  • Night before — note any scheduled macro events Fed speeches, CPI, jobs report, or major economic data scheduled for the next morning? These move the whole market at open. Know them in advance so you're not caught off guard.
  • Morning (as early as 6:00 AM) — check futures Open TradingView → ES1! (S&P futures) or NQ1! (Nasdaq futures). Are they significantly up or down? This tells you whether the open is likely bullish or bearish before the market opens. Sets your bias for the day.
  • 6:00–8:00 AM — early gapper watch (optional) Pre-market movers start forming as early as 6:00 AM on Finviz or Trade-Ideas. You can begin watching but don't commit to names yet — volume is too thin before 8:00 AM to be reliable. Watch only.
Pre-Market Routine — 8:00–9:25 AM ET
  • Find the gappers Open Trade-Ideas or Finviz pre-market screener by 8:00 AM. Filter: gap % > 3%, pre-market volume > 500K. These are your candidates for the day — nothing else.
  • Narrow to 2–3 names maximum Why is it gapping? News catalyst (earnings beat, FDA, upgrade) = tradeable. No news = skip. Mystery gaps have no edge.
  • Check options liquidity before open Go to Barchart. Open interest at your target strike > 500. Bid/ask spread < $0.20 on contracts under $2.00. If spread is wide, skip options — trade shares instead.
  • Mark pre-market high and low on chart These are your key levels for the first 30 minutes. A break above pre-market high = long signal. Break below pre-market low = short signal.
  • Set your max loss for the day before market opens Hard number. When you hit it, screen off. No exceptions. Scalping without a daily loss limit is a fast way to blow an account.
Market Hours Routine — 9:30–10:30 AM ET (Primary Window)
  • Do not trade the first 5 minutes (9:30–9:35) Market makers are filling overnight orders. Spreads are wide, moves are erratic. Watch only. Let the dust settle.
  • 9:35 AM: confirm direction Is price holding above or below the pre-market high/low? Is volume confirming the move (high rel volume = real move)? Both must agree before entry.
  • Entry: 1-minute chart only Stoch RSI crosses above 20 from oversold = long entry. Crosses below 80 from overbought = short entry. Must align with price direction from step 2.
  • ≤3 DTE options: target fast, exit faster These contracts move in minutes, not hours. Take 50–75% of your planned gain and exit. Don't hold for full target. Theta eats you alive by noon.
  • 10:30 AM: reassess or step away The best scalping setups resolve in the first hour. After 10:30, liquidity drops and moves become choppier. It's acceptable to be flat by 10:30 and done for the day.

✅ Setup Valid → Entry · Stop · Target · R:R

Once Valid/Invalid + S/R Lookback + VRVP all clear, here's the trade math. Same logic both directions — flip "above/below" for puts.

📍 Entry
Calls (long): at resistance break (price holding above) OR pullback to support that's holding.
Puts (short): at support break (price holding below) OR pullback to resistance that's holding.
Rule: at it and holding. Not "close to."
🛑 Stop Loss
Long: just below the support that held.
Short: just above the resistance that held.
Buffer 5–10¢ — wicks lie. Don't sit right on the level.
Set at entry order time — never mental.
🎯 Target
Calls: next resistance above entry.
Puts: next support below entry.
Same S/R rules — 3+ touches, drawn on daily.
Use S/R Lookback card to identify the next level.
📐 R:R Math
R = entry − stop (long) | stop − entry (short)
Reward = target − entry (long) | entry − target (short)
R:R = Reward ÷ R
≥ 2R → take. < 2R → skip.
Worked example — NVDA long Entry $118 (broke and held) · Stop $116.50 · Target $124 (next resistance).
R = $118 − $116.50 = $1.50 · Reward = $124 − $118 = $6 · R:R = $6 ÷ $1.50 = 4R · take it ✓
🌐 SMC Entry/Exit Lens — Sharpens LJ's Trade Math

⚙ Sister Checks — Earnings · Catalysts · Unusual Flow · OI

Four checks to run on your 2–3 finalists alongside ② IV Rank. Some happen in TradingView, some need Market Chameleon. Each block tells you WHAT to look at and what decision to make.

📅 Earnings
What: Date the company reports financial results.
Why it matters: Options pricing goes unpredictable around earnings — IV spikes, then crushes after. Your direction can be right and you still lose.
Where to look: TradingView's E-markers on your chart (already on). Hover for the exact date.
Rule: E inside 14–45 day DTE → skip or shorten DTE to expire before E. Hard kill.
Skip Market Chameleon for this — TV's E-markers are enough since you're never trading through earnings.
🎯 Catalysts — Which Actually Matter
The problem: TV's Economic Calendar shows hundreds of events daily. 95% are noise. Filter to four kinds that actually move your stocks:
① Fed / FOMC meetings — moves the WHOLE market. Especially XLF (financials) and rate-sensitive sectors.
Where: TV Economic Calendar → filter Importance: High → look for FOMC.
② CPI · NFP (jobs) · GDP — major macro releases. Moves SPY/QQQ broadly.
Where: TV Economic Calendar → High importance filter catches these.
③ FDA dates — ONLY if biotech/pharma. For NVDA/AAPL/MSFT/META = irrelevant, skip.
Where: Market Chameleon's FDA calendar, or biopharmcatalyst.com.
④ Stock-specific events — Apple events, Tesla delivery numbers, big contract announcements.
Where: news headlines on the ticker — you'll usually see these without a calendar.
Rule: High-importance macro event inside your DTE → plan to be flat before it, or skip the trade.
Everything else on the Economic Calendar (housing starts, ISM Services, foreign data) = ignore unless you've been burned by it before.
🐋 Unusual Options Volume — Institutional Positioning
What: Today's options volume on a strike that's WAY above normal. Example: AAPL 220 calls normally trade 500 contracts/day — today they traded 25,000.
Why it matters: Someone with deep pockets just placed a big directional bet. They might know something — or just be hedging. Useful as one input, not the deciding factor.
Where to look: Market Chameleon → top nav → Unusual Options Volume → search your ticker. Three columns to scan:
▸ Direction — calls (bullish) or puts (bearish)?
▸ Premium spent — large $ amounts = institutional, small $ = retail (skip)
▸ Vol / OI ratio — 5×+ = noteworthy
Rule: Match to your direction.
🟢 Flow agrees with your bias → confirmation, proceed
🟡 Flow opposite your bias → caution, look harder before entering
⚪ No unusual flow → neutral, proceed if other checks pass
Don't blindly follow institutional flow — they may be hedging existing positions, not making a directional bet. One signal, not the final word.
📊 Open Interest Changes — Crowd Agreement at Your Strike
What: OI = total contracts outstanding at a strike. Different from volume — volume = today's trades, OI = total contracts still open (never closed).
Why it matters: Rising OI at YOUR strike = other traders agree it's a meaningful level. Higher OI also = tighter bid/ask spread, easier to fill and exit.
Where to look: Options chain on Webull or Tastytrade (also on Market Chameleon's options page). Find the OI column at your target strike. Note today vs prior day.
Rule when picking a strike in card ③:
🟢 OI > 500 AND growing day-over-day → ideal
🟡 OI > 500 but flat or falling → acceptable, lower conviction
🔴 OI < 500 → skip (already a hard rule in ③)
Why two tools? Barchart owns the IV Rank number. TradingView owns earnings + macro. Market Chameleon owns the unusual-flow lens (+ FDA if you trade biotech). Each in its lane — don't cross-shop.
🔄 Reversal Setup — Alternative to LJ Trend-Follow

When the market is range-bound or no clean LJ setups exist, reversal plays profit from extremes snapping back. Different mindset, different recipe, different rules.

LJ Trend-Follow
"Ride the wave that's moving." Wait for 3 of 4 indicators to align WITH direction. Enter at S/R with the trend.
Reversal Play
"Fade the extreme that's exhausted." Wait for oversold/overbought to start turning. Enter against recent move, betting on snap-back.
✅ Use reversal mode when ❌ Skip reversal mode when
Market is range-bound (VIX low, SPY chopping) Strong trend in place — don't fight clean trends
LJ screens return zero clean setups across sectors "Catching a falling knife" without confirmation
Specific name showing capitulation (oversold + high volume bar) Pre-earnings / pre-catalyst — don't fade events
Post-news overreaction (gap already faded once) 4H MA Ribbon shows strong opposite-direction trend
⚙ Webull screen recipe
Base filters (same as LJ)US · $20–500 · Avg Vol 60D >5M · ATR >2%
IV Percentile50–90 (higher IV = more rebound potential — different from LJ's 20–70)
Pick ONE oscillator signal (don't stack)
Long reversal via Williams %RW%R < -80 AND turning up
Short reversal via Williams %RW%R > -20 AND turning down

Target: opposite end of recent range OR the 20 SMA on daily (whichever is closer).
R:R: aim for 2R+ minimum. Reversal trades are lower probability, need higher reward to compensate.

Options 101 — Fundamentals Reference

Strike selection (ITM/ATM/OTM), liquidity friction (bid/ask), and Open Interest. Reference these when picking a strike in the Swing tab's card ③.

📚 Options Fundamentals — Strike Selection · Liquidity · OI

Three concepts every options trade hinges on. Reference these when picking a strike in Swing → card ③.

🎯 ITM / ATM / OTM — Strike vs Stock Price
ITM (In The Money) = strike has intrinsic value · for calls: strike below stock · for puts: strike above
ATM (At The Money) = strike ≈ stock price
OTM (Out The Money) = pure time value · for calls: strike above stock · for puts: strike below
% = |Strike − Stock| ÷ Stock × 100 — how far the strike is from current price
Tradeoff matrix
Deep ITM (>5%)High cost · low leverage (moves dollar-for-dollar) · highest probability · capital-heavy
Slightly ITM (0–5%)Medium cost · balanced leverage · good when you want survival through choppy weeks
ATMAll time value · delta ~50 · 50/50 odds · balanced
Slightly OTM (0–5%)LJ's sweet spot — cheap, high leverage if right, matches "nearest resistance/support" rule
5–10% OTMCheap · very high leverage if right · needs big move within DTE
>10% OTMLottery ticket · usually expires worthless · skip unless catalyst-driven
P&L story — NVDA at $200, 30 DTE, $1,400 budget Stock +10% to $220: ITM (1cx, $1,300) = +$1,700 (+131%) · ATM (2cx, $1,400) = +$2,600 (+186%) · OTM (4cx, $1,400) = +$2,600 (+186%)
Stock +5% to $210: ITM = +$700 ✓ · ATM = +$600 ✓ · OTM = -$1,400 ❌ total loss
Stock flat at $200: ITM = -$300 (time only) · ATM = -$1,400 ❌ · OTM = -$1,400 ❌
Stock -5% to $190: all three = total loss (-$1,300 / -$1,400 / -$1,400)
Lesson: OTM wins big when you're right + big move. ITM survives when you're right but timing is slow. Stock has to actually move for ATM/OTM to make money.
📏 Bid/Ask <10% — Liquidity Friction Rule
Bid = what someone will pay to buy from you (you sell at this)
Ask = what someone will sell to you (you buy at this)
Spread = Ask − Bid · this is friction you lose the moment you enter
Examples — AAPL call
Bid $1.80 / Ask $2.00Spread $0.20 ÷ mid $1.90 = 10.5% — FAIL, skip
Bid $4.50 / Ask $4.70Spread $0.20 ÷ mid $4.60 = 4.3% — PASS
Bid $0.80 / Ask $1.10Spread $0.30 ÷ mid $0.95 = 31% — hard fail, you're already down 15% on entry
Why it matters: a 30% spread means you're down ~15% the instant you enter (you buy at ask, would only get bid back). That's a massive edge giveaway before the trade has moved.
Where to check on Webull: Options Chain — Bid and Ask columns visible per strike. SPY/QQQ/AAPL/NVDA weeklies usually 1–3% (penny-wide). Small-caps and far-OTM strikes often blow past 10% — skip those.
📊 Open Interest (OI) — Crowd Position at Your Strike
OI = total contracts outstanding at a strike. Different from Volume:
• Volume = today's trades only (resets daily)
• OI = all contracts still open (built up over time)
How to read OI at your strike
High OI (>500) + growing day-over-dayIdeal — crowd agrees on this level · tight spreads · easy fills
High OI but flat/fallingAcceptable · lower conviction · positions unwinding
OI < 500Skip — illiquid · wide spreads · hard to exit at fair price
OI building at strikes above current priceBullish positioning · institutions expecting upside
OI building at strikes below current priceBearish positioning · hedging or directional shorts
Practical use: when picking a strike in card ③, favor strikes where OI is >500 AND growing. High OI = liquid = you can exit when you want, not when the market lets you. Check on Webull options chain (OI column) or in the Top Options tab to see where the heaviest positioning is.
🔴 Live Trading — Entry · Monitoring · Exit

When you're about to enter, already in, or deciding to exit. Three cards, one job each. Glance, act, close.

🟢 Entry — Pulling the Trigger

After Setup & Math confirmed the trade. Now you're at the chart, ready to place the order. Three timeframes in sequence, then fire.

Sequence — Daily → 4H → 1-min
Daily (last check)Confirm price is at your S/R level — not approaching, AT it. No E-marker (earnings) within DTE.
4H (last check)Still 3 of 4 indicators aligned? MA Ribbon · RSI 45–55 rising · MACD bullish/bearish · Stoch K position.
1-min (trigger candle)Pull the trigger here. Wait for the cross.
📈 LONG — 1-min trigger
• Stoch RSI K crosses above 20 from oversold
• Price holding above your S/R level
• Volume bar green and bigger than average
• SMC: SmartTrend Entry signal fires
📉 SHORT — 1-min trigger
• Stoch RSI K crosses below 80 from overbought
• Price holding below your S/R level
• Volume bar red and bigger than average
• SMC: SmartTrend Entry signal fires
⚡ Order Placement — Bracket / OCO
Order type: Bracket / OCO (entry + stop + take-profit in one ticket)
Entry: Limit order at the mid or 1¢ favorable
Stop loss: Just below swing low (longs) or above swing high (shorts) · 5–10¢ buffer
Take profit: T1 = first FVG fill or next S/R
Submit — never enter without the stop attached.
After fill: close the 1-min chart, switch to 4H or Daily, watch Positions tab. Full reference: Setup & Math → Entry/Stop/Target/R:R.
👁 Monitoring — In the Trade

Your bracket order is doing the watching. You just need to spot setup invalidation or approaching targets. Do not watch the 1-min mid-trade.

✅ Watch on 4H + Daily
• 4H: still 3 of 4 indicators aligned?
• Daily: setup not invalidated · S/R intact · no E-marker approaching
• Frequency: pre-market · ~30 min before known catalysts · end of day
❌ Do NOT watch
• 1-min · 5-min mid-trade — noise will panic-exit you
• Random news in unrelated tickers
• P&L every 5 minutes — refresh-anxiety kills trades
What each 4H signal means for an open trade
3 of 4 indicators still alignedHold · stay in · stick to plan
1 indicator started disagreeing (e.g., RSI hit 75)Note it · still hold unless 2+ flip
2+ indicators flip against youReal warning — consider exit at next bounce/dip
Price closes back through your S/RSetup invalidated — manual exit, don't wait for stop
Approaching T1 targetBe ready to close 50% · trail stop up to lock in profit
Catalyst day approaching (Fed/CPI/earnings)Decide BEFORE: hold through or close prior · don't be caught by surprise
The discipline: the whole reason we set up bracket orders is so you DON'T have to babysit. Stop is automated. T1 is automated (if you set it). You're free to live your life — check 4H twice a day, that's it. If you find yourself on the 1-min mid-trade, that's your tell that you're trading the emotion, not the system.
🛑 Exit — Three Ways Out

Every trade ends one of three ways. Plan all three before entry. Pre-defined exits = good trader. Reactive exits = bad trader.

✅ Target hit (planned)
• T1 hit → close 50%
• T2 hit → close another 30%
• T3 hit → close last 20%
• OR trail stop up to lock in profit, let it ride
🛑 Stop hit (automated)
• Bracket order does this for you
• Don't second-guess — accept the loss
• The stop existing IS the system working
• No revenge re-entry — log it, move on
⚠ Manual exit (invalidation)
• 4H trend flipped (2+ indicators against)
• Daily close back through your S/R
• News changed the picture
• DTE ≤5 days, not at target → exit before theta crush
Cardinal sins — never do these
Move stop further awayTo "give it room" — that's hope, not strategy. The trade is invalidating. Take the loss.
Hold past invalidationHoping it comes back. The system says exit. Stop hoping, start acting.
Exit early on noise2–3% pullback on the 1-min ≠ invalidation. Stay on 4H. Don't look at the 1-min.
Re-enter to make it backRevenge trading. Every revenge trade compounds the loss. Log the L, take a break, screen tomorrow.
Add to a losing positionAveraging down on options = doubling the loss. Cut, don't average.
From LJ's IWM lesson: "You can't just let it go to zero. Bad habit. Cut it, don't just let it go all the way down." Mental stops fail under pressure — that's why we use bracket orders. If the bracket isn't there and you're watching it bleed, close manually now, don't hope.
