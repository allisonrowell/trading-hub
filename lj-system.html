Analysis Flow — Always Top-Down
every trade
Step 1
Day Chart
Ground zero. Sets overall bias — bullish or bearish. Always start here.
Step 2
4-Hour Chart
Confirms intermediate trend. 2 candles on 4hr = 1 day candle.
Step 3
1-Minute Chart
Times the entry. Only after Day bias confirmed.
Result
Trade or Skip
All align → trade. Conflicting → skip or drop timeframe.
MACD domino effect: MACD crossovers cascade across timeframes — 4hr crossover precedes Day, Day precedes Week. They all work into each other. A pop from one timeframe's crossover pushes the stock higher, pulling the next timeframe's MACD closer to crossing. Use this sequence to time entries.
Timeframes to pin in TradingView: 1 min · 4 hr · Day · Week. Trade on 1-min. Mark up on Day first. Use 5-min if 1-min feels too fast — it's slightly slower but still valid.

Stop Loss — LJ's Exact Words
every trade
"You can't just let it go to zero. Bad habit. Cut it, don't just let it go all the way down."
A mental stop that you don't act on is not a stop. It's hope. Getting stopped out is the point — protection, not failure. Many traders avoid stops because it kicks them out of trades. That's the whole point.
RuleDetail
Place at entry
Set stop loss order at the same time you enter — not after it moves against you.
Mental stops fail
IWM trade (June 15): mental stop at -$20. Didn't act. Position dropped ~95%. Cost: $50–60. The stop existed. The execution didn't.
Mo's approach ≠ yours
"She doesn't do stop losses — she IS a stop loss." Different risk profile. Don't inherit someone else's style.
At -$20 / 30-40%
That was the mental stop. That was when to get out. The moment you said you would get out is the moment to get out.
0DTE decay
Zero DTE options decay extremely fast. If it's going against you on a 0DTE, it is NOT coming back. Cut immediately.

Copyable Rules
Indicator conflict rule
2 bullish / 2 bearish → skip the trade or drop to a lower timeframe. 3-to-1 is tradeable. Never force when the stack disagrees. No trade is a valid trade.
Stop loss rule
Never let a trade go to zero. Cut it. Don't watch it burn. Place stop loss at entry — not mentally. A mental stop you don't act on is hope, not a stop.
Entry timing rule
Do not trade the first 5–10 minutes after market open. No structure yet. Wait for candles to form support, resistance, and pattern clarity.
Analysis flow
Day chart → bias. 4-hour → confirms. 1-minute → times entry. Always top-down. Never skip to 1-min without Day confirmation first.
S/R level validity
At least 3 touches. Draw at candle color changes and clusters — indecision zones. Not single-touch spike highs or lows. Mix of bodies and wicks is correct.
Retest rule
After a breakout, always expect a retest. Price coming back to confirm the broken level = locking the door. Normal. Not a reason to panic-exit.
Strike selection
Calls → nearest resistance level as strike. Puts → nearest support level as strike. Going long = longer expiration to give trade time to reach target.
Late alert protocol
Feel late on LJ's alert → ask: "What's your average?" Near his avg = still valid entry. Far above his avg = wait for next setup. Can also ask "Is this trade still good?"
Stoch RSI entry rule
Wait for K-line to cross above 20 before entering from the downside. Just because it's at 0 doesn't mean it crosses 20 immediately. Crossing 20 = confirmed momentum. Above 80 = sharp reversal risk — last leg of the race.
Weekend gap rule
Stock up big overnight or over weekend → people will take profits at open → puts opportunity. Volume coming in = people selling their winners. Wait to confirm direction before entering.
